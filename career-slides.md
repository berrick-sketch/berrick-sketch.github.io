# AI and the Future of Screenwriting

Alexandra Berrick  
AI 109  
Career Focus: Screenwriting for Film and Television  
May 1, 2026

# Career Snapshot

- Screenwriters create scripts for films and TV episodes
- Core work: concepts, outlines, scenes, dialogue, revision
- TV writing emphasizes collaboration in writers' rooms
- Why this career: creativity, storytelling, and cultural impact

# Skills That Matter Most

- Story structure and pacing
- Character arcs and emotional stakes
- Dialogue and subtext
- Collaboration and feedback response
- Professional communication (loglines, pitches, one-pagers)

# AI Disruption: What Could Change

- AI can draft basic outlines and scene options quickly
- Some entry-level development tasks may be automated
- AI script coverage tools may reduce traditional reader roles
- Faster tool output may increase deadline pressure

# AI Disruption: Industry Risks

- Potential pressure toward formulaic storytelling
- Legal and authorship disputes around generated material
- Confidentiality and data security concerns in script development
- Need to understand labor protections and contract language

# AI Benefits for Writers

- Faster brainstorming when ideas stall
- Research assistance for first-pass background learning
- Revision support for clarity and continuity checks
- Quicker adaptation of pitch documents for different audiences

# How to Gain an Advantage

- Use AI for support tasks, not for replacing original authorship
- Build repeatable workflows for drafting and revision
- Keep strong focus on voice, emotional truth, and nuance
- Show ethical and policy literacy about AI in creative work

# One-Year Action Plan

- Draft one original TV pilot and one feature outline
- Run three revision cycles for each project
- Build and test an AI-assisted workflow checklist
- Track WGA and media-law updates on AI monthly
- Practice concise pitch materials for each script idea

# Main Takeaway

AI will likely change *how* screenwriters work, but human writers remain essential for voice, meaning, and emotional depth.  
The strongest future screenwriters will combine storytelling craft with responsible AI fluency.
